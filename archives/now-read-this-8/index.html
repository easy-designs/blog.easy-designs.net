Now Read This VIII

Posted by Aaron Gustafson on 29 August 2011 | Permalink

Last week, Steve Jobs resigned and Twitter launched a resource for quickly building prototypes and apps. Here’s the roundup of those and other links you’ll want to check out:

  • OMB Extends Ban On New Federal Websites
    Agencies must list the sites they’ve already shut down and describe how they’ll improve the remaining ones.
  • At O’Hare Airport, Unused Land Is Going to the Bees
    What an awesome program. I’d love to see something like this take root at CHA.
  • Experimenting with line-height in input fields
    I love it when browsers save us from ourselves.
  • Across the Globe, Solar Power Just Keeps Getting Cheaper
    I can’t wait until solar for the home becomes more affordable. That… and geothermal.
  • 360° Long Road (Zurich)
    Amazing use of video, panoramas and audio. Bravo!
  • Turntable Kitchen’s Pairings Box - Food & Music, together at last!
    Fingers crossed Kelly will think this is as cool as I think it is.
  • US nuke plants ranked by quake risk
    Hooray, Sequoyah (here in Tennessee) is number 4.
  • Jobs resigns as Apple CEO
    You knew it would happen one day.
  • Bootstrap
    A great new resource from the folks at Twitter.
  • FlexSlider
    A responsive jQuery slider.
  • Graceful Degradation and How it Tricks You
    A great post from Neils Matthijs.
